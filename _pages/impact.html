From an overview, the merit of pre-lecture, lecture and post-lecture material might not be obvious. However, the Hake Gain shows measurable improvement of student comprehension and retention of basic physical principles.

Hake Gain

To quantify student learning, the Hake Gain compares student results on identical pre- and post-course surveys. Suppose that B and A are mean student scores before and after instruction, respectively; the Hake Gain is defined as H = (A-B)/(M-B), where M is the maximum possible score. Note that students who start and end with high scores show little gain so, results are not skewed by students with prior understanding. From previous studies, a Hake Gain of 0.52 ± 0.10 is possible with an active learning approach, compared to 0.22 ± 0.05 for traditional didactic lectures

Measurable Improvement

As seen in a histogram, the modal Hake Gain for my students climbed steadily for three successive years. In the first year of active learning (2015) my students achieved a Hake Gain of 0.26 ± 0.02 compared to 0.10 ± 0.02 when I gave traditional didactic lectures the previous year (2014). In just one year, learning more than doubled!
