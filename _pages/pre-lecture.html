In the 24 hours before class, students are expected to watch a brief pre-lecture video that shows physics in action. Students can also download a student version of the lecture notes.

Pre-lecture Video

Each pre-lecture video provides an essential empirical prelude to development and application of theory, in lectures. I created a custom interface that solicits and grades student responses. To favor limbic association over effortful memory, I let students fix mistakes immediately. For the YouTube generation, "snacking" on short videos is more appealing than assigned textbook readings.

The following example provides a demonstration of Lenz's Law, setting the stage for a lecture, tutorial and assignment that follow.

Lecture Notes

There are two versions of the lecture notes, one for students and one for instructors.

Student Version

Before each lecture, students can download a student version of the notes. The student notes are a redacted version of the instructor notes, with annotations and key words removed.
